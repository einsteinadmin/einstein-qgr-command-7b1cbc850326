How the Company Did

Q2 in Plain English

What — Record revenue, profit behind plan

We did $8.4M in revenue — up almost 10% over last year, the biggest quarter in company history. And we didn't just ride a good market: the moving markets around us grew about 2–5% — we grew at roughly 3× the market's pace. We hit ~95% of our revenue plan. Profit came in at about two-thirds of what we planned — record revenue did not mean record profit this quarter. (July numbers are estimates until books close ~Aug 1.)

So What — The profit gap was mostly spending we chose, and a lot of it was spent on you

When we dug into the miss, it wasn't waste and it wasn't crews costing too much — total mover pay actually came in under budget. Here's what happened: May demand started slow, and instead of cutting crews back, we held the full summer crew build — we kept hiring and kept hours steady so we'd be ready when the season hit, rather than running you thin and scrambling. We also invested in things that don't pay off in the same quarter: leadership coaching, a stronger finance team, and a bigger marketing push (billboards, mailers). Add those up and they explain most of the gap. The honest miss on our side: we didn't forecast those investments well — that's on leadership, and fixing our forecasting is part of why the finance investment exists.

Meanwhile the company stayed strong where it counts: we bought 17 trucks this quarter (4 net-new, 13 replacements) and still generated cash — revenue covered our bills roughly 1.5 times over. Nobody should read this quarter as "the company is in trouble." It isn't.

Now What — Book more of the work we're already built to serve

We proved this quarter that we have the people, the trucks, and the leads (leads nearly doubled). The constraint was turning leads into booked jobs — and that's exactly where Q3 points. For movers: keep availability up and keep NPS up — utilization and reviews are how your branch earns its next truck and your next promotion slot opens. For CERs and sales: speed-to-lead and follow-up on the thousands of new leads the quote form now captures. Your branch's specific picture is on your branch scorecard — same link family as this page.

What

Following Distance events fell 54% — the quarter's biggest safety win. Total events rose only because we deliberately tightened two standards (Rolling Stop, Inattentive).

If revenue is a record, where did the money go?
Mostly chosen investments: early summer staffing, coaching, finance team, marketing — plus 17 trucks.
Is the company okay on cash?
Yes: cash-generating quarter, bills covered ~1.5×, and the truck buys were mostly bank-financed.
Will prices go up to fix the profit gap?
Pricing runs on its own weekly cadence; nothing in this quarter's story is a price-panic move.
My Branch
Why does my branch look bad on the scorecard when we worked our tails off?
Effort shows in the tiles you control (NPS, safety, availability); some misses this quarter were target-math or market issues, and your BM has the branch-specific read.

Turnaround plans with their RM; growth elsewhere means moves and slots across the network, not cuts.

How does my branch get another truck?
Utilization + booked demand: run the trucks you have full and convert your leads, and the fleet plan follows.
Leads / Sales
Leads doubled — why didn't revenue double?

Is conversion down? I heard the rate dropped.
