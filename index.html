How this machine works
Warehouse → shortlist → showroom → write-back
(1) The Master Backlog is the warehouse: every idea and project (~500 items), evidence attached as updates, deduped 7/20; the QGR-Lens tag surfaces items into its "QGR Q3 Review" view.
(2) The rock draft is the shortlist: Albert curates the warehouse down to per-leader rock candidates. Curation weights, in order: cross-leader convergence at Rob's constraint session (Flywheel + PACe lenses) → Rob's coaching reads → Q2 carries (explicit kill/carry/restructure, nothing auto-carries) → MB lens/priority flags → hard rules (2 CRITs each, Matisen 4-cap, dependencies named). It is judgment, 70–80% right by design — which is why every pass ships with a curation receipt (per-rock provenance + the near-misses and why not) for the Cameron+Paul alignment pass: audit the deltas, never the whole warehouse.
(3) The Triage Room is the showroom: cards seeded from the shortlist only; kill/edit/promote decisions happen there and export back to Albert.
(4) Write-back: every decision lands back in the Master Backlog + cascading sheet — nothing stays decided only inside a tool. Feedback loop on this HQ itself: ✎-note anything, Export, hand Albert the JSON (or say it in chat) — folded in + redeployed same session, changelog records it.
The four beats of every QGR cycle (locked 7/20 — the Runway phases mirror this)
① Collect + review. Starting ~3 weeks out, gather every input the quarter's decisions need: People Analyzers, leader AARs, eNPS and customer feedback, financial and marketing analysis, branch scorecards where the data allows — plus the strategy instruments themselves: the all-leader Flywheel/PACe constraints exercise (~2 weeks out; this is where the quarter's biggest issues surface), 7 Strata, SWT, and the CASh/Power-of-One cash review. Everything lands in the Master Backlog until it is genuinely complete.
② Inputs → warehouse → triage (Cameron + Paul). First, every instrument that FEEDS the backlog runs: SWT refresh, 7 Strata + FACe, the PACe/Flywheel self-evaluation, CASh/Power-of-One (its 1%-moves become backlog candidates), and the OPSP left page. Then the explicit warehouse gate: fold all outputs in, declare the Master Backlog complete, regenerate the shortlist + curation receipt.
The decision loop after the gate (proven live 7/20 — run it in this order):
1. Albert curates the shortlist + regenerates the curation receipt (per-rock provenance + near-misses).
2. Cameron + Paul rule the receipt (confirm/challenge rocks, promote/keep/kill near-misses) → Export decisions → Albert digests.
3. Albert folds the rulings into the Master Backlog — the "Q3 Curation" status column marks every pulse ⭐ Shortlisted / ✔ Kept / 🔀 Folded / ✖ Killed — and writes the coverage audit (every high-signal pulse the curation never touched).
4. Cameron sanity-checks the backlog against the audit ("what got missed?") — misses land in the Triage Room's overflow bands or shortlist; this is also the graded test of how well the curation weights worked.
5. Triage Room red-pen: per-leader lanes (shortlist + overflow + Cameron-adds), owners assigned at BOTH tiers (Roundtable + their managers: Nhel/Tet/Carly/KP/Fabian/Jalissa/Prosper/Austin L), CRIT counts enforced → Export → Albert writes decisions back to the MB per the grade-sync crosswalk.
Sequencing rule: lock the WORKING theme hypothesis before assigning goals (the theme is the triage lens) — but the theme's name/mechanics/rewards stay a team decision at the QGR. Target exiting this beat: 7–8 goals per leader, manager tier sketched, refined down during ③–④.
Theme at a high level, leaders' general goals, packet mostly locked by EOQ. Purpose-built tools carry the weight because raw-backlog triage is too heavy: the shortlist + receipt + Triage Room.
③ Confirm (Matisen data day → readiness). Before leadership walks in: dashboards, Games scoring, and branch data verified clean; a final gut check across goals, overlaps, and cross-leader dependencies; the QG packet finished and ready to present.
④ Decide + cascade (Leadership QGR). The team reviews 7 Strata, SWT, People Analyzers, last quarter's results, and the packet together — then finalizes everything that was only sketched before: each leader's goals with exact Green/Super-Green definitions, their directs' goals with measurables, the theme with its mechanics and rewards, and the OPSP + Vision Summary. The output feeds straight into the following week's cascade: manager, mover, and CET presentations (Phase ④ on the Runway).
Note the deliberate double-touch: owners populate the strategy artifacts in ①–②, leadership reviews and finalizes them in the room — same documents, two passes, not duplication. Customer-input beyond GatherUp is deliberately deferred until regular customer outreach exists.
