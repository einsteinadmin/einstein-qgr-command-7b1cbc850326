How this machine works
📋 Warehouse→ 🪨 Shortlist + receipt→ 🛠 Triage Room→ ↩ Write-back
The Master Backlog holds everything (~500 items) · Albert curates it to per-leader rock candidates with a receipt · kill/edit/promote happens in the Triage Room · every decision lands back on the board. Nothing stays decided only inside a tool.
The full loop — curation weights, receipt logic, feedback rules
(1) The Master Backlog is the warehouse: every idea and project (~500 items), evidence attached as updates, deduped 7/20; the QGR-Lens tag surfaces items into its "QGR Q3 Review" view.
(2) The rock draft is the shortlist: Albert curates the warehouse down to per-leader rock candidates. Curation weights, in order: cross-leader convergence at Rob's constraint session (Flywheel + PACe lenses) → Rob's coaching reads → Q2 carries (explicit kill/carry/restructure, nothing auto-carries) → MB lens/priority flags → hard rules (2 CRITs each, Matisen 4-cap, dependencies named). It is judgment, 70–80% right by design — which is why every pass ships with a curation receipt (per-rock provenance + the near-misses and why not) for the Cameron+Paul alignment pass: audit the deltas, never the whole warehouse.
(3) The Triage Room is the showroom: cards seeded from the shortlist only; kill/edit/promote decisions happen there and export back to Albert.
(4) Write-back: every decision lands back in the Master Backlog + cascading sheet — nothing stays decided only inside a tool. Feedback loop on this HQ itself: ✎-note anything, Export, hand Albert the JSON (or say it in chat) — folded in + redeployed same session, changelog records it.

The decision loop after the gate (proven live 7/20 · RE-SEQUENCED 7/21 — run it in this order):
1. Albert cleans the warehouse — only after ALL inputs are folded in (AARs, eNPS exports, Flywheel/PACe outputs, CASh 1%-moves): dedupe, two-layer summaries on every pulse, lens columns, priority drafts, curation receipt (per-rock provenance + near-misses), coverage audit ("what did curation never touch?").
2. Leadership audits + stamps their groups (Kit step 4, ~20–30 min each): reality-check every pulse, fix priority/lens/ownership columns, emoji-vote their own rock candidates. Only owners can verify freshness — no walk starts until the stamps are in.
3. Cameron + Paul walk the warehouse (~1.5–2 hrs, recorded): audit + confirm priorities THROUGH the lens views, rule on Albert's curation calls and the leaders' stamps, work the coverage audit as the exception list; transcript to Albert, corrections folded back into the board. ④ Decide + cascade — the Roundtable QGR and the front line
④ Decide + cascade (Leadership QGR). The team reviews 7 Strata, SWT, People Analyzers, last quarter's results, and the packet together — then finalizes everything that was only sketched before: each leader's goals with exact Green/Super-Green definitions, their directs' goals with measurables, the theme with its mechanics and rewards, and the OPSP + Vision Summary.
