📗 Scaling Up "Cash" — the Einstein edition (2-minute primer)

The core question: how fast does a spent dollar come back as a collected dollar? Most companies wait 30–60 days on receivables. Einstein collects at move completion — zero AR. So of the classic "Power of One" seven levers, only four are live here:

LeverEinstein reality
Price +1%This IS Variable Pricing. 1% on ~$24M ≈ $240K straight to the bottom line. Cleanest lever.
Volume +1%Conversion + truck utilization — more moves through trucks already paid for ≈ nearly pure margin.
COGS −1%= labor. The dominant cost. Gross margin is a labor-utilization story: paid hours vs. billable contract hours.
Overhead −1%Insurance, paid ads, branch overhead — Tampa is the live case study (high GM, negative NI).
AR days / Inventory days / AP daysMostly moot — no receivables, no product inventory, small vendor terms. Don't spend analysis here.

The one real "money in transit" lag we do have: invoice closure speed (mobile app fixes this) and the lead→booked cycle. And with no AR, a 13-week rolling cash forecast here would be unusually accurate — bookings ≈ near-certain cash on known dates.
